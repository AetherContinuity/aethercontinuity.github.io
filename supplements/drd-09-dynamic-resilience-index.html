Dynamic Resilience Index (DRI)

Integrating C2-CI · MRI · ORM · CDSI into a composite national resilience metric

Abstract. DRD-09 introduces the Dynamic Resilience Index (DRI) — a composite metric integrating four previously separate indices: C2 Cohesion Index (C2-CI, DRD-03/06), Mobility Resilience Index (MRI, DRD-08 §02c), Operational Readiness Multiplier (ORM, CN-014/015), and Cross-Domain Synergy Index (CDSI, CN-015). DRI measures not static defence capacity but dynamic capability — the system's ability to absorb, adapt and recover under operational stress. The key structural finding: static resilience (E, D, platform inventory) is necessary but insufficient. ORM and CDSI — the dynamic, operationally-derived components — determine whether static capacity can be activated under pressure.

§ 02

DRI Composite Formula

DRI is a multiplicative composite — not additive. A system with high static capacity (MRI) but low operational readiness (ORM ≈ 0) cannot activate that capacity under stress. The indices multiply rather than compensate:

DRI = C2-CI · MRI · √(ORM · CDSI)

The geometric mean of ORM and CDSI captures their joint effect: operational readiness is only valuable if it is coordinated across domains (CDSI), and cross-domain coordination only produces resilience if crews are genuinely trained through real operations (ORM).

§ 05

Limitations and Calibration Notes

  • All component estimates are proxy-based. DRI v1.0 is a diagnostic framework, not a precision instrument. Values should be treated as order-of-magnitude indicators.
  • C2-CI estimates draw on DRD-03's qualitative assessment; empirical calibration requires structured droneincident and exercise data.
  • ORM calculation requires reliable logging of actual vs. exercise flight hours — this data is not currently published in a form suitable for DRI calculation.
  • CDSI is the most subjective component; it will benefit from the CN-015 ohjausryhmä process producing structured assessments of inter-agency coordination quality.
